Scale Wheel

The scale wheel is a draggable circular object that contains the basic notes of the scale and markers indicating the distance between the notes (half step or whole step).
Half step graphic:
Whole step graphic:

You can drag the circle to the right or left to choose the mode (indicated at bottom of screen). In essence the mode dictates the note you begin a scale with. Then touch the letters of the scale to hear the sound of the scale.

Modes

  1. Ionian
  2. Dorian
  3. Phrygian
  4. Lydian
  5. Mixolidian
  6. Aeolian
  7. Locrian

Scale Selector

The scale selector (at the top) allows you to change the scale you want to display/hear.

When a new scale is selected the mode always starts out as Ionian

The fretboard is a display of the frets of the guitar. The display shows 5 strings of the guitar and the first several frets relative to the scale you've chosen. The grey dots are specific positions on the fretboard in which you can find notes of the scales. The fingering of this scale position is referred to as major 6-1.

When you touch a note on the scale circle, not only does the sound of that note play, but the various fret positions where you can find that note are shown in bold on the fretboard.

You can also touch the grey circles to hear the notes.
